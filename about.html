About Me

I am a Florida-based student pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Science at the University of South Florida, with a strong interest in data analysis, information systems, and technical problem-solving. I have always been highly detail-oriented and naturally drawn to recognizing patterns, organizing information, and working through complex problems in a systematic way. These strengths, combined with my academic background and professional experience, have shaped my goal of pursuing a career as an information professional in a data or analytics-focused role. Throughout the BSIS program, I developed a strong foundation in understanding how information is created, structured, managed, and utilized within digital environments. My coursework emphasized working with data across its lifecycle, from collection and organization to cleaning, analysis, and interpretation. I gained experience analyzing structured datasets, identifying trends, and cleaning, organizing, and drawing meaningful insights from imperfect or incomplete data. This process strengthened my problem-solving skills and reinforced the importance of precision and attention to detail when working with real-world information. In addition to conceptual knowledge, the program provided hands-on experience with technical tools and software commonly used in the field. I worked with programming and analysis tools such as R, SQL, and Java, along with data visualization techniques to communicate findings clearly and effectively. Learning to debug code, refine analyses, and improve outputs helped me become more comfortable navigating technical challenges and adapting when initial approaches did not work as expected. These experiences reinforced my ability to think critically, remain organized, and approach problems methodically, especially when working with complex systems or large datasets. My academic experience is complemented by professional work in customer-facing and management roles, where I developed strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills. In management positions, I was responsible for coordinating daily operations, delegating tasks to and managing team members, and ensuring all processes ran efficiently and accurately. These responsibilities required clear communication, organization, and the ability to make informed decisions while balancing multiple priorities. Working closely with both customers and team members strengthened my ability to adapt my communication style to different audiences and situations. I regularly identified problems, gathered relevant information, and implemented practical solutions, often under time constraints. These experiences reinforced the importance of collaboration, empathy, and clarity, particularly when addressing issues that involved both technical systems and human needs. My management background also helped me develop confidence in leadership, accountability, and professional judgment, skills that directly support effective decision-making, coordination, and communication in all types of information and communication-based roles. Across both academic and professional experiences, I have consistently demonstrated strengths in organization, pattern recognition, and analytical thinking. I am comfortable working with structured information, maintaining detailed records, and identifying inconsistencies or opportunities for improvement within systems. I also value clear documentation and thoughtful workflows, as these practices support accuracy, transparency, and long-term usability of information. These skills allow me to approach information challenges with unique and well-rounded perspectives. Looking ahead, my short-term career goal is to secure an entry-level role in a data- or information-focused position where I can gain hands-on experience and continue developing my technical and analytical skills. I am particularly interested in opportunities that involve working with data, improving processes, and supporting data-driven decision-making within organizations. Gaining real-world experience will allow me to apply the concepts learned in my coursework while further strengthening my ability to work with complex information systems in professional environments. In the longer term, I aim to grow into more advanced analytical or technical roles such as IT project management by continuing to build my expertise in data analysis, programming, and information management. I plan to pursue ongoing learning through additional training, certifications, and practical experience to stay current with evolving tools and industry needs. Ultimately, my goal is to contribute meaningfully as an information professional who brings strong analytical skills, attention to detail, and thoughtful problem-solving to organizations that rely on accurate, well-structured information.
